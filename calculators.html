2D Principal Stresses

A 2D element can experience stresses (σ) and shear
stresses (τ). At a certain orientation, the element can
experience maximum stresses and shear stresses.

This calculator will be able to provide the average normal
stress, the principal stresses (σ1 and σ2) and the angle
they occur at, and the maximum shear stress (τ) and
the angle it occurs at.

Cylindrical Pressure Vessel Stress

The pressure vessel can be capped or uncapped.
Stresses can be calculated on the inner or outer walls.

A carbonated drink is an example of a capped/closed
pressure vessel, while a gun barrel is an example of an
uncapped/open pressure vessel.

If the vessel is uncapped/open, there will not be an
axial stress.

Column Failure

The point of failure for a column under compression load
can be determined after finding the slenderness ratio,
which will determine if the Euler(long-length) or
Johnson(intermdiate-length) equation will be used.

This calculator will be able to determine the critical stress,
critical load, and the factor of safety.

Strength and Cold Work

Cold working a material will change its strengths.
A new yield strength will be produced, as well as
a new ultimate tensile strength due to a reduction in area.

Some examples of a cold working process are extrusions
through a die, shearing a material, and running a
material through two rollers.
